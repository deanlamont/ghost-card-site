How It Works

Four steps. Even the guy with the Bluetooth speaker can manage it.

Ghost Card is a disc golf scoring app. Pick a course, add players, score live, haunt your history. Here's exactly how.

01

Pick your disc golf course

Ghost Card has 7,008 US disc golf courses indexed and searchable by name, city, or zip code. Like the opening scene of a horror film — you choose the location, and then you live with that decision for 18 holes.

Par data is community-verified where we have it. Spot a wrong par? Submit a correction. Five corrections unlocks the Bone Collector bag tag. Hole 4 is a par 4. The sign has always been wrong.

02

Build your scorecard

Add real players, invite friends to join, or drop in ghost pros from the professional field — real PDGA ratings, real names. Adam Hammes (rated 1029) is on the list. He will not speak. He will just score clean 47s until you respect him.

Stroke Play, Scramble, or Solo. MPO or FPO. In Scramble mode, enter a team name — it goes on the card. Your dad and a ghost Simon Lizotte is a reasonable group. Your dad and ghost Ricky Wysocki (1044) is a learning experience. Want a multi-round event? Tap Custom Tournament under the Format section. Want chaos? Enable Horror Mode — a 50-card card game that runs alongside your round.

03

Score your round

One-hand input designed for the field — the other hand is holding a disc, a coffee, or whatever dignity you have left after hole 6. Running +/− from par updates live. The leaderboard reshuffles after every hole.

Three ghost layers compete alongside you: pro ghosts (cream), your personal best (gold), and your friend's best round (cyan) — all using real hole-by-hole score data. Watch your lead disappear in real time. Very motivating, clinically speaking.

04

Haunt your round history

Every round saved permanently. Full hole-by-hole breakdown. Every score, every bad lie, every "the wind got it" — all on record. Your GC Rating is calculated after every round. The disc golf scoring app does not forget. The ghosts do not forget.

Share your round badge. Track your rating trend across your last 20 rounds. Check the Activity Feed to see what your friends have been playing. Review any previous card. In horror films, the evidence is always there if you look.
