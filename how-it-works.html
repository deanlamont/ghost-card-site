How It Works

Four steps.
Even the guy with the Bluetooth speaker can manage it.

In every horror movie there's a moment where the character should turn back. This is yours. You're still here. Good. Let's play.

01

Pick a course

7,008 US courses. Search by name, city, or zip. Like the opening scene of a horror film — you pick the location, and then you have to live with that decision for 18 holes.

Par data is verified where we have it, community-corrected where we don't. Submit a correction and you're on the path to the Bone Collector tag. Hole 4 is a par 4. The sign is wrong. It's always been wrong. The sign is the villain.

7,008 Courses Par correction docs
02

Build your card

Add real players or drop in ghost opponents from the pro field — real PDGA ratings, real names. Adam Hammes is on the list. He shoots clean 47s without breaking a sweat, never speaks, and makes eye contact the entire round. Like a horror villain, but with better form.

Stroke Play or Best Disc Scramble. MPO or FPO. Mix real players with ghosts. Your dad and a ghost Simon Lizotte is not weird. Your dad and a ghost Ricky Wysocki is a bad idea. Ricky will destroy him. We've seen it.

Stroke Play · Scramble · MPO · FPO Card builder docs
03

Keep score

Hole-by-hole input designed for one hand — the other one's holding your disc, your coffee, or whatever's left of your dignity after hole 6. The leaderboard updates live. Every. Single. Hole.

Running +/- from par updates live. The leaderboard reshuffles after every hole. Watch your position deteriorate in real time. Very motivating, clinically speaking.

Live Leaderboard Scoring docs
04

Haunt your history

Every round saved. Full hole-by-hole breakdown. Every score, every bad lie, every "the wind got it" that nobody believed. All on record. Forever. The app does not forget. The ghosts do not forget. You will try to forget.

Share your round badge. Review any previous card. In horror films, the evidence is always there if you look. That's what round history is. You already know what happened on hole 7. You just need to see it in writing.
