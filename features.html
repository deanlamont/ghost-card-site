Features

Everything you need. Nothing that's going to make a slideshow about it.

No synergy. No ecosystem. No guy explaining his disc selection for four minutes on the tee pad while you die inside. Just the features of a disc golf game that actually earned the word — including a full DGPT-style season, a 50-card horror card game, and 134 ghost pros who will never make you wait on the tee.

Core Feature

Solo vs. Pro Field

Real PDGA ratings. Ghost opponents score based on actual stats. When Ricky beats you by 11 it's not personal. It's just what happens when you invite a monster to your card. He doesn't even know your name. The ghosts do.

Game Format

Scramble

Everyone throws, group plays from the best disc. One team score per hole. Enter a team name — it goes on the scorecard, the leaderboard, and the results card with your player names listed underneath. Ghost opponents scramble too, sorted into teams. Good for when your dad is on the card and the horror movie has a "we survive together" moment.

Multi-Round

Custom Tournament

Name your tournament. Pick 1–5 rounds, Stroke Play or Scramble, up to 4 players. Choose a different course before each round starts — two rounds at Cedar Beach, two at Mossy Bluff, whatever you want. Ghost pro teams get horror-themed names that randomise per tournament. Cumulative leaderboard updates after every round. The Uekman Classic is a real thing now.

Season Mode

Tour Mode & Career Story

A full 10-event season — Regular events, Elite+, a Major, and a Season Finale worth double points. DGPT-calibrated points structure. Standings update after every event. Ghost opponents compete in the standings alongside real players.

Your career narrative runs underneath it. First season you're nobody. Win events, win seasons, and the story changes. By Season 5 the field has heard of you. The ghost card is yours to lose — or defend.

Horror Mode · Multiplayer · 2+ Players

Horror Mode — The Card Game

A 50-card card game overlay that rides on top of any multiplayer round. Enable it at card build and a full horror deck enters the round alongside your scorecard. Cards are drawn, played, and resolved in real time — during the round, on the course, hole by hole.

Four card types: Haunt your opponents, play Spirit cards on yourself, hold Reckoning cards until the final hole, burn Wild cards when things get desperate. Skins run alongside. Whatever happened on hole 9 stays on hole 9.

New · Social

Friends & Activity Feed

Add friends by username, see their recent rounds in a dedicated Feed tab. The feed shows course, score, GC Rating, and full scorecard breakdowns on tap. Head-to-Head view auto-surfaces courses you have both played and compares your best scores side by side.

Mutual friendships only. 20-friend cap. No follower counts, no likes, no algorithms. Just rounds and who played them. The ghosts do not do engagement metrics.

New · Leaderboard

Beat Your Ghost — Personal Best

Your previous best round at the current course appears as a gold ghost row on the live leaderboard — labeled YOUR BEST and tracking hole by hole with real score data. Not simulated. Not estimated. That was you, on that day, and now you have to beat yourself.

Set a new personal best and the Round Complete screen celebrates with a full comparison: holes won, holes lost, holes tied, strokes gained. The ghost noticed.

New · Friend Ghost

Friend Ghost on Your Leaderboard

When you play a course a friend has played before, their best round appears as a cyan ghost row on your live leaderboard — right next to the pro ghosts and your PB ghost. Real hole-by-hole score data. If they birdied hole 7, you will see it on hole 7.

Three ghost layers on one leaderboard: pro ghosts (cream), your personal best (gold), your friend (cyan). The solo round was never really solo.

New · Analytics

Rating Trend & Share Badge

A sparkline chart on the History screen tracks your GC Rating across your last 20 rated rounds. Green line means you are getting better. Orange means the ghosts are winning. Your best-ever rating is marked. Tap any point to see the number.

Every completed round generates a shareable badge image — course, date, score, GC Rating, the whole resume. Export it. Post it. Prove you were there. The ghosts do not need to prove anything.
