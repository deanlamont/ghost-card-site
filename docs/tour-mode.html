06 — Tour Mode

Multi-round events.
A season of hauntings.

Overview

Tour Mode is Ghost Card's multi-round tournament system. Where a standard round is a single scorecard, Tour Mode strings multiple rounds together into an event and awards points based on finish position. Events roll up into seasons, which track overall standings.

Tour Mode is modeled loosely on the DGPT (Disc Golf Pro Tour) points structure — not identical, but using the same logic of awarding points per event finish and accumulating them into season totals.

Sign-in required

Tour Mode requires a signed-in account. Seasons and event history are tied to your account and stored in Supabase. Guest mode cannot access Tour Mode.

Event types

Ghost Card has four Tour Mode event tiers, each with different round counts, point multipliers, and stakes. A full season uses all four.

Regular

Regular Event

3 rounds. 100 points for 1st. The baseline. Most events in a season are Regular events. The ghosts take them seriously anyway.

Elite+

Elite+ Event

3 rounds. 125 points for 1st. A 1.25× multiplier on all positions. Higher stakes, same round count. Mid-season inflection point.

Major

Major Event

5 rounds. 150 points for 1st. A 1.5× multiplier. The season’s marquee event. Winning a Major means something specific.

Finale

Season Finale

5 rounds. 200 points for 1st. A 2× multiplier. The final event — worth double. Standings can flip entirely.

A default Ghost Card season runs 10 events: Events 1–5 and 8–9 are Regular, Event 6 is Elite+, Event 7 is the Major, and Event 10 is the Season Finale. You can mix courses across rounds — each round in an event can be played at a different course.

Points structure

Points are awarded per event based on finish position with a DGPT-calibrated structure. Each event tier multiplies these base values. Ghost opponents compete in the standings alongside real players.

Seasons

Events belong to seasons. A season is a named collection of events — you create a season, add events to it, play through them, and track standings across the full season schedule.

To start a season:

  • 1
    Open Tour Mode — Tap the Tour tab from the bottom nav.
  • 2
    Create a season — Name your season. The name is yours — be creative or be functional, the ghosts don't care. "2026 Haunt Circuit" works. So does "Season 1."
  • 3
    Add an event — Choose Standard (3 rounds) or Major (5 rounds). Name the event, or accept the default name.
  • 4
    Play the rounds — Each round in the event uses the standard card builder. Build your card, play, score, save. The event accumulates results.

Standings

Season standings update after each event completes. The Tour tab shows current standings with points totals per player across all events in the season. Ghost opponents appear in standings alongside real players — the standings don't distinguish between them.

Standings persist as long as the season is active. You can have multiple active seasons simultaneously.

Custom Tournament Mode

Custom Tournament Mode is separate from the Tour season system. Where Tour Mode uses a fixed 10-event season calendar with DGPT-style points, Custom Tournament Mode is a user-created multi-round event — you name it, set the format, pick the player roster once, and choose a different course before each round starts.

Access it from the Play tab: tap the gold Custom Tournament card that appears under the Format selector.

Stroke Play

Individual scoring

Each player has their own score. Cumulative standings track individual totals across all rounds — like a real stroke-play tournament.

Scramble

Team scoring

One score per hole for the team. Enter a team name — it goes on the card and the standings. Great for you and your dad vs. the Disc Reapers.

  • 1
    Create the tournament — Name it, pick Stroke Play or Scramble, set your division, add up to 4 players, pick 1–5 rounds, and set field difficulty. These settings carry through every round.
  • 2
    Pick a course before each round — After finishing and saving a round, the app routes you back to a course picker for the next one. Cedar Beach round 1, Mossy Bluff round 2 — your call every time.
  • 3
    Save each round — Rounds save to your History just like any other round, tagged to the tournament. The "Play Next Round" button only appears after you save.
  • 4
    View standings after each round — Tap "View Tournament Standings" on the finished screen. The cumulative board shows round-by-round scores and totals — the same grid format you'd see in a real tournament.

Ghost pro team names

In Scramble Custom Tournaments, ghost pro teams get horror/disc golf themed names — "The Disc Reapers," "Bogey or Die," "Chainshot Chainsaw," "The Unholy Rollers." Names are randomised per tournament but stable across rounds within it — same teams every round, different tournament next time.

Active and completed Custom Tournaments are listed in the Tournament Hub — tap "Custom Tournament" on the Play tab to see them. Resume an in-progress tournament or view results of a completed one at any time.

Career Story

Tour Mode runs a career narrative underneath the season structure. This is not optional and does not need to be enabled — it runs automatically from your first Tour Mode round.

The narrative has four tiers, earned by winning seasons:

  • 1
    Newcomer (0 wins) — "The leaderboard doesn't know your name. The field doesn't pause. You're in now. That's all the tour owes you."
  • 2
    Contender (1–2 wins) — The field is starting to notice. The ghosts are paying closer attention. You are no longer a surprise.
  • 3
    Veteran (3–4 wins) — "The leaderboard remembered your name from last season. The field noticed." Returns get a narrative moment before the new season begins.
  • 4
    Legend (5+ wins) — "Five seasons. Five times at the top. The ghosts stop following — they watch." The tour understands you now.

Career narrative moments appear at key inflection points: your first Tour Mode entry, your first top-10 finish, your first event win, your first Major win, each season championship, each new season start, and season-end standings. They are written in Ghost Card's standard deadpan horror comedy voice and cannot be disabled.

Season naming

Your first season is auto-named using your career name — something like "Chadley — Season 1." Subsequent seasons increment automatically. You can override the name during season creation.
