Tour Mode & Career Story

A full season.
A career on the line.
The tour remembers everything.

Tour Mode runs a 10-event season — Regular events, Elite+, a Major, and a Season Finale worth double points — using a DGPT-calibrated points structure. Standings update after every event. The ghosts compete in the standings alongside real players. Ricky Wysocki is in the standings. He is fine with this.

Your career narrative runs underneath it all. First season, you're nobody. The leaderboard doesn't know your name. Win events — win seasons — and the story changes. By Season 5, the field has heard of you. The ghost card is yours to lose.

Multiplayer & Playing With Friends

Real players. Ghost pros.
Your round, your group, your rules.

Ghost Card is not just a solo game. Invite real Ghost Card users into your round, mix them with ghost pros however you want, and optionally turn the whole thing into a horror card game. Your group's leaderboard is live. The ghosts don't care who you brought. They're keeping score regardless.

Invite Real Players

Invite signed-in Ghost Card users to your round by name. They see the invite on their end and jump straight into the shared scorecard. You score your holes, they score theirs — one live leaderboard for everyone. Up to 4 players per card, including ghost pros.

Ghost Pros in the Mix

Real players and ghost pros live on the same card, same leaderboard. You and your dad vs. ghost Gannon Buhr and ghost Paul McBeth is a completely legitimate group. The ghosts play statistically. They don't slow the group down. They don't explain their lines. They throw and they score. That's it.

Local Man Accepts Fate By Hole 6

UNNAMED COUNTY — A local disc golfer, identified only as Chad, 41, reported Tuesday that he had "fully accepted his life" by the sixth hole of a round in which he had invited ghost Gannon Buhr onto his card. Buhr, rated 1027, birdied the opening hole while Chad was still mid-sentence explaining to a companion why he throws a Nuke OS on a 180-foot par 3. Authorities confirmed no foul play. The disc was found in the rough.

Joey Buckets Drains 60-Footer; Player Six Feet Away Misses

TENNESSEE — Ghost pro Joey Buckets converted a 60-foot putt on hole 12 of a competitive round last Saturday. The player described their current condition as "recovering, slowly." Ghost Buckets offered no statement, as ghosts do not speak.

Ghost Card Observer — Saturday Edition Breaking
Eleven Strangers Camp Hole 4; Ghost Field Plays Through Without Comment

A group of eleven individuals conducting what sources described as a birthday round occupied hole 4 of a public course at approximately 2pm on a Saturday, blocking play for an estimated duration of "forever, basically." A solo player in the group behind, unable to proceed, opened Ghost Card and added a full ghost field to their card. The ghost pros teed off immediately. They played through the group. They did not ask permission. They did not make eye contact. They simply scored. The solo player, reached for comment, stated only that "the ghosts understand me in a way real people don't." They were given a Ghost Card bag tag for completing the round. The eleven strangers remain unaware of any of this.

Solo player  ·  Always  ·  Everywhere  ·  ★★★★★
